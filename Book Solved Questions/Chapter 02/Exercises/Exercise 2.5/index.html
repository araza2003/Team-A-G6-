Exercise 2.5

I recommended that two spaces between sentences be produced by using an &nbsp; reference followed by a space (Section 2.3.4). Why is this approach to producing two spaces between sentences better than either using a space followed by &nbsp; or using two &nbsp; references? (Hint: Think about how the sentences will appear as the browser window is resized.) Your answer should not only describe how the sentences might appear, but also explain why.

Answer

The recommended approach of using &nbsp; followed by a regular space ensures proper text wrapping behavior as the browser window is resized. Here's why:

  • &nbsp; followed by a space: The non-breaking space ensures that the first word and the space stay together, while the second space allows natural wrapping if needed.

  • Space followed by &nbsp;: The normal space allows wrapping, but if the &nbsp; is at the end of a line, it might cause inconsistent spacing.

  • Two &nbsp; references: This approach prevents line breaks between the sentences, forcing them to stay on the same line and potentially leading to poor text flow.

Thus, using &nbsp; followed by a space ensures both proper spacing and flexible text wrapping as the browser adjusts.
